THE USUAL PROBLEM

Being in a band already comes with enough moving parts.

Writing songs. Rehearsing. Booking shows. Making flyers. Posting updates. Chasing ticket links. Loading gear. Sharing clips. Planning releases. Answering messages. Trying to keep the next thing moving while everyone has jobs, lives, and different levels of group-chat awareness.

Then the internet asks for more.

Show dates need to be current. Links need to point to the right place. Clips need to get posted before they disappear into someone's camera roll. Booking info needs to be easy to find. Release pages need to make sense. Tour dates need clean details. And whatever you are building online still needs to feel like your band, not a generic template wearing your logo.

When that stuff gets scattered or stale, people can hear your name, like your set, search you later, and still not know where to listen, where to follow, who to contact, or when to see you again.

We help clean up the trail around what you are already doing.

Sometimes that means a simple page. Sometimes it means show links and tour updates. Sometimes it means content help. Sometimes it means building something that fits the moment, like a temporary MySpace-style tour page with show dates that automatically move into past shows after the date passes.

The point is not to make you look like every other band.

The point is to make sure people can find the version of you that already exists in the room.

How this works

  1. Choose where you need backup

    Pick the option that sounds closest: cleanup, a better home base, show support, content help, or ongoing digital support. You do not need to know the perfect package before reaching out. The form just helps us start in the right place.

  2. Send what you have

    Share your band name, website, socials, streaming links, show dates, flyers, videos, or whatever exists right now. Messy is fine. Half-updated is fine. "We meant to fix this months ago" is extremely normal.

  3. We check the online trail

    We look at what people actually find: your website or link page, socials, show dates, ticket links, streaming links, videos, booking info, and anything that helps someone decide to listen, follow, book, or show up.

  4. We build around the band

    You get practical recommendations and options that fit your actual band, not a generic template. Maybe that is a simple link page. Maybe it is show dates that archive themselves. Maybe it is social help, live clips, release support, or a weird little themed page for one specific run.

Home page show widget

A small show widget can live on your main band website or landing page and show the next few upcoming dates without taking over the whole page.

It can show the next 3 shows, including date, city, venue, age restriction, and ticket or info links. A "View all shows" button takes fans to the full tour page.

Best for

Bands that already have a website but need their upcoming shows to be easier to find right away.

Standard tour page

A full tour page gives your band one reliable place for show dates, ticket links, venue details, lineups, and past show history.

The design can still match your band's vibe. It might use live video footage in the background, band photos, release artwork, or colors and textures that fit the current era.
